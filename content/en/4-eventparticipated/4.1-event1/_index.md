## Event Report – AWS Community Day June 2026

### Purpose of the Event

Attending AWS Community Day June 2026 gave me the opportunity to learn about the latest technology trends in Cloud Computing and Generative AI. The event offered not only expert knowledge on AI, Cloud, and modern application development, but also the chance to network and learn from industry experts and the broader tech community.

Through the sessions, I gained a clearer understanding of how businesses are applying AI in practice, modern AWS services, and career paths in Cloud and AI.

### Speaker List

- Tinh Truong – Speaker, AI & Context Engineering
- Anh Pham – Speaker, Amazon Quick
- Thinh Nguyen – Speaker, Amazon CloudFront
- Team VIB – LotusHacks Hackathon Team
- Duc Dao – Speaker, Large Language Models
- Vy Lam – Speaker, Enterprise AI Systems

### Highlights

**1. Context Is Everything: Making AI Actually Work for You**

This session helped me realize AI only truly works well when given full context. The speaker shared many real examples of AI giving wrong or inaccurate answers due to insufficient input information and overly generic prompts.

The concept of a "Second AI Brain" impressed me the most — the idea of building an AI assistant capable of remembering information and supporting users over the long term, rather than only responding to a single isolated command. The speaker also introduced methods for Context Engineering and Memory to improve AI response quality, and guided the audience on developing AI Agent applications.

**2. Friendly AI Assistant with Amazon Quick**

In this session, I learned about the Amazon Quick toolset and how AI is applied to unlock data. Notable features include:

- Quick Chat Agent — supports data queries in natural language.
- Quick Flows — helps build automated workflows without coding.
- Quick Spaces — supports storing and sharing organizational knowledge.
- Quick Sight — helps build dashboards and reports from raw data using AI.

This session showed me that AI can support not just programming but also data analysis and enterprise management in a powerful way.

**3. From Edge To Origin: CloudFront as Your Foundation**

The speaker introduced Amazon CloudFront and the role of a CDN in optimizing application performance. I learned more about:

- How CloudFront reduces latency and speeds up access.
- Cost optimization through a distributed caching system.
- Enhanced security through HTTPS and AWS WAF.
- Improved load tolerance and system availability.

This part was especially useful to me since it directly relates to the ZeroBug Agent project I'm working on, and I could see how CloudFront could be applied to my own system.

**4. 36 hrs with LotusHacks – Building UTMorpho from Idea to Reality**

This was one of the most inspiring sessions for me. Team VIB shared their journey of building a product within 36 hours at a Hackathon competition. Through their presentation, I learned:

- How to find an idea that fits a real-world problem.
- Teamwork skills under high pressure.
- How to manage time and divide work effectively.

These experiences motivated me further when working on personal projects and my graduation thesis.

**5. Non-Determinism of "Deterministic" LLM Settings**

This session explained how large language models select the next token, and why results can still differ even when Temperature is set to 0. The speaker also analyzed:

- The factors causing model non-determinism.
- The effect of inference optimization.
- Methods to reduce deviation when building AI applications.

Although I did not fully grasp everything shared in this session, it was still a fresh and interesting area of knowledge for me.

**6. Enterprise-Grade Multi-Agent System: The Case of Startup Credit Scoring**

The final session introduced a Multi-Agent System architecture applied to startup credit scoring. What impressed me most was the "Virtual Credit Committee" model, in which multiple AI Agents collaborate to reach a decision rather than relying on a single model. The speaker also shared:

- Real-world experience and working practices, and the importance of security in the banking environment.
- The practical deployability of a Multi-Agent System.
- The cost and operational efficiency benefits.

**Mindset on AI**
- Context is decisive for the quality of AI systems.
- AI is shifting from Prompt Engineering to Context Engineering and Memory.
- Multi-Agent Systems are the new development trend for enterprise AI applications.

**Technical Architecture**
- Understood the role of Amazon CloudFront in optimizing performance and security.
- Grasped the architecture of AI Agents and Multi-Agent Systems.
- Understood the non-deterministic nature of large language models.

**Product Development Skills**
- MVP-oriented product-building mindset.
- Teamwork and time-management skills.
- How to apply AI and Cloud to real-world problems.

**Application to My Work**

After attending the event, I plan to apply some of what I learned to my studies and work, such as:
- Researching the use of Amazon CloudFront to optimize performance and security for the ZeroBug Agent system.
